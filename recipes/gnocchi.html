Gnocchi Recipe

Description

Small pieces of dough, usually round of oblong in shape that are boiled in water or broth and then tossed with different sauces.
Can be made from flour or potatoes, they are perhaps one of the most ancient homemade pastas in the world.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds baking potatoes (about 4)
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • Pink Himalayan salt
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, plus more for dusting
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Freshly grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese
  • Freshly grated nutmeg

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350Â°F. Place potatoes directly on middle rack in preheated oven. Bake until very tender when pierced
    with a knife. A bit over 1 hour.
  2. Carefully cut potatoes in half lengthwise; let stand until cool enough to handle. Use a paring knife to peel potatoes
    and discard the skins.
  3. Thoroughly mash potatoes in a bowl and place on a clean work surface; spread in an even layer.
    Let cool until almost room temp, Drizzle egg yolk evenly over potatoes.
  4. Sprinkle salt and 1/2 cup of the flour evenly over potatoes and egg yolk. Using a bench scraper or rubber spatula,
    very gently fold mixture over onto itself several times. Gradually fold remaining 1/2 cup of flour until dough is
    incorporated and dough comes together in mass.
  5. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and dust lightly with flour. Divide dough evenly in 4 pieces,
    Roll 1 dough piece into 3/4 inch thick rope. Cut rope crosswise, roll each piece against tines of a fork to make ridges;
    transfer to baking sheet and repeat process with all dough.
  6. Bring salted water to a boil in a 4-quart stockpot over high. Cook until gnocchi rise to surface, about 2 minutes,
    and then gently boil 1 more minute, reducing heat as necessary to maintain a gentle boil.
  7. Melt 3 tablespoons of butter in large skillet. Transfer cooked gnocchi to skillet over medium-high heat.
    Cook about 1 minute until a creamy and loose butter sauce forms and coats gnocchi. Season with salt and pepper.
